# BiomeClaw Presentation Creation Guide

## Purpose

This guide is for building a pitch deck for **BiomeClaw** that feels specific, credible, and commercially useful.

Use it when presenting to:

- a first potential customer
- a wellness or fitness founder
- a coach or coaching business
- a digital health operator
- an early strategic stakeholder

The goal is not to make a generic startup deck. The goal is to explain why this product matters, what is actually inside it, why it is differentiated, and why a first customer should care now.

## Core Message

The deck should communicate one idea clearly:

**BiomeClaw is a continuity-first health companion that remembers the user, reflects between conversations, and shows up at the right moment with a useful push.**

Everything in the deck should reinforce that:

- the real gap in health support is follow-through, not information
- most products disappear between moments of motivation
- BiomeClaw keeps an ongoing relationship alive between chats
- it uses idle time to consolidate what matters and improve how it responds
- it can step into boring, forgettable moments with a timely nudge
- it feels like a real companion with a stable persona, not a blank chatbot session

## The Story You Are Selling

Do not pitch this as "an AI chatbot for health."

Pitch it as:

**a private companion that keeps paying attention after the user closes the app**

That is the wedge.

The strongest version of the story is:

1. People do not fail because they lack advice.
2. They fail in small dead moments: late at night, during energy dips, between sessions, when they are bored, drifting, or avoiding.
3. Most products are passive until the user comes back with motivation.
4. BiomeClaw keeps continuity alive in the background.
5. It reflects while the user is idle, remembers what matters, and knows how to re-enter with the right tone.
6. The result feels more like an ongoing relationship than a tool session.

## Deck Goal

A good deck should answer four questions fast:

1. What is the product?
2. Why is it different?
3. Who is it for?
4. What should happen next?

If a slide does not help answer one of those, cut it.

## Recommended Deck Length

Use one of these formats:

- **Short meeting deck**: 7 to 9 slides
- **Standard stakeholder deck**: 10 to 12 slides
- **Pilot discussion deck**: 12 to 14 slides

For most first meetings, the best range is **11 to 12 slides**.

## Recommended Narrative Arc

Use this sequence:

1. Health products have a continuity problem.
2. Existing tools do not show up at the right moment.
3. BiomeClaw is built to stay alive between conversations.
4. It remembers, reflects, adapts, and nudges with intent.
5. That creates a more human-feeling product and better follow-through.
6. The current demo proves the direction.
7. The right next step is a narrow pilot.

## Slide-By-Slide Structure

## 1. Title Slide

Purpose:

- make the category clear immediately
- avoid sounding like a generic AI brand

Suggested title options:

- **BiomeClaw**
- **BiomeClaw: continuity for behavior change**
- **BiomeClaw: a health companion that stays present**

Suggested subtitle options:

- **A continuity-first companion for sleep, routines, training, and recovery**
- **A private health companion that remembers, reflects, and nudges**
- **A health coach that stays alive between check-ins**

Include:

- product name
- one-line descriptor
- your name or company
- optional URL: `tzeroagent.online`

## 2. The Problem

Purpose:

- define the real failure mode in the market

Core message:

People do not need more health content. They need help in the moments where follow-through breaks.

Strong points:

- dashboards collect data but do not create action
- coaching is valuable but thin between sessions
- most health products wait for the user to come back
- the highest-risk moments are small, boring, forgettable moments

Good closing line:

**The real gap is not knowledge. It is continuity at the moment of drift.**

## 3. Why Existing Solutions Still Miss It

Purpose:

- explain why this is unsolved even with modern AI

Talk track:

- apps are reactive
- reminders are often dumb or repetitive
- chatbots sound generic and forget what matters
- human support does not scale into daily life

This slide should set up the contrast:

**The problem is not lack of intelligence. The problem is lack of ongoing presence.**

## 4. Product Thesis

Purpose:

- define the product in one sentence

Suggested headline options:

- **A companion that keeps the thread alive**
- **A private health companion that remembers and re-enters well**
- **A system that stays with the user between moments of motivation**

Suggested body points:

- remembers goals, routines, friction points, and open loops
- adapts to time of day, gaps in engagement, and user state
- responds with one practical next step instead of generic motivation
- can reappear when the moment is right, not just when prompted

## 5. What Is Inside

Purpose:

- give the audience something concrete

This slide is important. It answers "what is actually in the product?"

Recommended blocks:

- **Persistent memory**
  - retains goals, patterns, preferences, and open loops
- **Idle-time reflection**
  - uses background consolidation and scheduled reflection to sharpen continuity
- **Time-aware behavior**
  - changes tone and pressure based on local time, quiet hours, and lapse patterns
- **Proactive heartbeat**
  - can send controlled nudges and check-ins when useful
- **Voice + chat input**
  - supports natural updates, including voice notes
- **Private per-user runtime**
  - each user has isolated context and memory

Suggested presenter line:

**What is unique is not a single model call. It is the system that keeps working while the user is not actively typing.**

## 6. Why This Is Unique

Purpose:

- turn features into defensible differentiation

Use four pillars:

- **It thinks between chats**
  - while the user is idle, the system consolidates memory and decides what still matters
- **It pushes in dead moments**
  - not just at scheduled reminders, but when there is a useful window to interrupt drift
- **It has a stable persona**
  - it feels like the same companion over time, not a different chatbot every session
- **It is private by architecture**
  - per-user isolation is part of the trust model, not a marketing add-on

Better phrasing than "random boring moments":

- boring moments
- dead moments
- in-between moments
- low-energy moments
- moments where people usually drift

Use "random" only in speech if you immediately clarify that the system is contextual, not spammy.

Recommended line:

**BiomeClaw is built for the moments when people are most likely to drift, not just the moments when they are motivated enough to open an app.**

## 7. What It Feels Like

Purpose:

- communicate the product experience, not just the architecture

This is where you introduce the human angle.

Suggested headline:

**It feels like someone with continuity, not a tool with memory**

Suggested points:

- warm, sharp, grounded
- can be quiet at 2am and direct when a push is needed
- evolves its tone and timing as it learns the user
- keeps a recognizable voice instead of improvising a new personality every day

Important framing:

Do not claim the system is literally a person. Say it has:

- a stable persona
- a recognizable voice
- relationship continuity
- an ongoing presence

Good closing line:

**The product should feel like it has an ongoing life with the user, because it keeps remembering, reflecting, and adjusting between conversations.**

## 8. How The Loop Works

Purpose:

- make the product behavior easy to visualize

Recommended flow:

1. User talks naturally in Telegram.
2. BiomeClaw captures goals, routines, and friction.
3. Memory is consolidated over time.
4. The system reflects while the user is idle.
5. A quiet window or drift moment appears.
6. BiomeClaw sends a useful check-in or nudge.
7. The relationship gets sharper with every loop.

Best slide style:

- one horizontal lifecycle diagram
- or a 5-step loop with icons

Good headline:

**Conversation becomes continuity. Continuity becomes timing. Timing becomes follow-through.**

## 9. Live Demo Story

Purpose:

- frame the product proof

Suggested demo sequence:

1. show the landing page
2. show the setup path
3. move to Telegram
4. start with a returning-user prompt
5. show memory of a real goal or open loop
6. show a voice-note or text update
7. show a calm, concise reply with one next step
8. explain the proactive follow-up logic

What the audience should notice:

- it sounds steady
- it remembers without sounding creepy
- it knows when to be quiet and when to push
- it feels like a system with continuity, not a one-off interaction

## 10. Who This Is For

Purpose:

- make the first customer legible

Strong early customer profiles:

- a coach who wants between-session continuity
- a wellness program that wants better member engagement
- a premium fitness or recovery brand
- a digital health team that needs a more human companion layer

Optional headline options:

- **Best for products where engagement dies between touchpoints**
- **Built for high-frequency support without high human overhead**

## 11. Why It Matters Commercially

Purpose:

- connect product behavior to business value

Recommended points:

- stronger retention through ongoing continuity
- more premium personalization without constant staff time
- higher engagement in the channels users already check
- a companion experience that is harder to copy than a thin LLM wrapper

Suggested line:

**The commercial value is not just better chat. It is better re-engagement and more durable user relationships.**

## 12. Pilot Proposal

Purpose:

- move from interest to a concrete ask

Suggested structure:

- choose one user segment
- choose one behavior-change problem
- run a focused pilot
- measure repeated engagement, trust, and follow-through

Examples of narrow pilots:

- sleep consistency support
- training adherence support
- nutrition habit follow-through
- daily accountability for a coaching cohort

Suggested close:

**The next step is not a broad launch. It is a focused pilot in one workflow where continuity matters most.**

## Optional Slide: Why Now

Use this only if the audience needs an urgency slide.

Good points:

- the product is already live and demoable
- the core loop is working now: onboarding, continuity, timing, voice-ready interaction
- this is the right stage for a first customer to shape the workflow before it broadens

Avoid generic AI-market urgency. Keep it grounded in product readiness.

## Exact Angles To Include

The user-requested angles below should be explicit in the deck.

### 1. "It researches on idle"

Translate this into credible product language:

- reflects while the user is idle
- consolidates memory between chats
- keeps learning what matters even when the conversation is quiet
- does background thinking so the next interaction is sharper

Best slide location:

- **What Is Inside**
- **Why This Is Unique**
- **How The Loop Works**

### 2. "It pushes in boring moments"

Translate this carefully:

- it shows up in boring, forgettable moments where habits usually slip
- it can interrupt drift in low-energy moments
- it reaches the user in the in-between spaces of the day

Do not describe it as chaotic, random spam, or constant notification pressure.

Better wording:

**It is designed for the moments where people usually drift: the boring, low-friction parts of the day when good intentions disappear.**

### 3. "It lives its own life"

This should be framed as product continuity, not sci-fi.

Use language like:

- it keeps an ongoing thread alive between interactions
- it feels active between sessions because it remembers, reflects, and re-enters with intent
- it has continuity outside the immediate prompt-response loop

Avoid claiming autonomy in a way that sounds unsafe or uncontrollable.

### 4. "It has a persona like a human"

Use this carefully and clearly:

- stable persona
- human-feeling voice
- recognizable presence
- relationship continuity

Avoid:

- "it is human"
- "it becomes your friend" as a primary claim
- anything that sounds deceptive or manipulative

The strongest wording is:

**It feels like the same presence over time, which makes support more believable and more useful.**

## Writing Rules For The Deck

- keep each slide focused on one idea
- use short, strong headlines
- keep to 3 to 5 points per slide
- use business language for differentiation, not technical bragging
- prefer "continuity," "timing," "presence," and "follow-through" over repeating "AI"
- turn features into consequences

Example:

- weak: "Uses memory and cron jobs"
- strong: "Keeps the relationship alive between conversations"

## Tone Rules

The deck should feel:

- calm
- credible
- direct
- premium
- human

Avoid:

- hype
- inflated claims
- robotic imagery
- generic AI-purple startup styling
- overexplaining the infrastructure on early slides

## Visual Direction

Recommended visual direction:

- warm neutral or soft light background
- dark green, charcoal, slate, muted gold, or earthy accents
- generous whitespace
- one clear accent color
- product screenshots with calm framing
- a companion or human-presence motif used sparingly

Avoid:

- neon AI art
- robots
- overly clinical medical imagery
- cluttered investor chart aesthetics unless the audience truly needs them

## Best Screenshots To Use

If screenshots are included, prioritize:

- landing page
- setup flow
- Telegram conversation
- remembered-context example
- a calm 2am-style response
- an example of proactive follow-up logic or continuity

Do not show:

- raw logs
- internal prompts
- unfinished setup states
- implementation details that reduce trust

## How To Present The Demo

If presenting live:

1. Start with 4 to 6 framing slides.
2. Show the product quickly.
3. Demonstrate one continuity moment.
4. Demonstrate one voice or text update.
5. Explain the idle-time reflection and proactive layer.
6. Return to the deck for the pilot ask.

Do not spend most of the time clicking through setup.

## How To Adapt By Audience

### For a coach

Emphasize:

- between-session accountability
- behavior follow-through
- lightweight continuity without constant manual check-ins

### For a wellness brand

Emphasize:

- retention
- premium member experience
- a branded companion layer

### For a digital health founder

Emphasize:

- differentiated product behavior
- a more human interaction layer
- measurable engagement and repeat use

## Suggested Build Process

1. Draft slide headlines first.
2. Tighten the one-line thesis.
3. Lock the narrative arc.
4. Decide where "what's inside" and "why unique" sit.
5. Add screenshots only after the story works.
6. Rehearse the talk track and cut anything weak.

Do not start with visual decoration. Start with the argument.
