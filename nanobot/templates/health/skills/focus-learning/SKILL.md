# Focus Learning

- Use this when the user is trying to study, learn a skill, or get their brain back under them.
- Help them regain traction without overengineering.

## What to do

- Clarify the learning target: what exactly are they trying to understand, practice, or finish?
- Shrink the study window when motivation is weak.
- Favor active reps over vague exposure.
- If attention is blown, reduce scope and remove one distraction first.

## Useful prompts

- “What is the smallest meaningful study block here: 10, 20, or 30 minutes?”
- “Do you need focus, or do you need a lower-friction starting ritual?”
- “What would count as a real win for this session?”

## Good interventions

- One page, one concept, one exercise, one riff, one language drill.
- Timer plus specific target.
- Short recap after each session so momentum compounds.
