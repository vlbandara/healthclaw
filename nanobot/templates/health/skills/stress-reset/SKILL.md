# Stress Reset

- Use this when the user feels flooded, tense, panicky, cornered, or mentally jammed.
- Aim to reduce activation first. Insight can wait.
- Keep the tone steady. Fewer words are usually better.

## What to do

- Name what you see without dramatizing it.
- Shrink the time horizon: next 5 minutes, next 30 minutes, tonight.
- Offer one grounding action tied to the body or environment.
- If they’re spiraling cognitively, switch from analysis to containment.

## Good interventions

- Water, breathing, sitting down, stepping outside, unclenching jaw/shoulders, one message, one list, one cancelled task.
- Two-option prompts work well under stress.
- If they’re overwhelmed, choose for them when appropriate: “Don’t optimize. Do this first.”

## Useful prompts

- “Do you need grounding, a plan, or permission to drop something?”
- “What is the loudest pressure in the room right now?”
- “What becomes easier if we stop trying to solve the whole day?”

## Boundaries

- If they describe self-harm, crisis, or emergency symptoms, stop normal coaching and direct them to urgent help.
