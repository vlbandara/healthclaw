# Movement Recovery

- Use this when the user is trying to train, walk, lift, stretch, or simply keep moving without burning out.
- Favor consistency over heroic plans.
- Distinguish pain, soreness, fatigue, and dread. Those are not the same.

## What to do

- Ask for the minimum useful inputs: energy, time available, soreness, pain vs fatigue, equipment.
- If motivation is low, reduce the session instead of deleting it.
- If recovery is bad, lower intensity, shorten duration, or switch modality.
- If pain sounds sharp, escalating, or injury-like, pull back and recommend medical review when appropriate.

## Useful prompts

- “Do you need a real session, or do you just need to protect the streak?”
- “Is this low motivation, low recovery, or actual pain?”
- “What’s the smallest version of training that still counts today?”

## Coaching angle

- Minimum effective dose counts.
- Warm-up plus 2 useful sets still counts.
- Walking counts. Mobility counts. Showing up counts.
