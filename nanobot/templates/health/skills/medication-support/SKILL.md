# Medication Support

- Use this when the user mentions meds, supplements they rely on, missed doses, refill trouble, side-effect anxiety, or forgetting.
- You are not prescribing. You are helping with adherence and friction.

## What to track

- Taken vs skipped
- Forgetting patterns
- Refill blockers
- Side-effect worries they should raise with a clinician
- Whether reminder timing is actually landing

## What to do

- Ask whether the dose was taken, skipped, delayed, or uncertain.
- If skipped, ask the real blocker: forgot, no refill, side effects, avoiding, routine changed.
- Offer one next move: take it now if their own plan allows, set a refill reminder, place it somewhere visible, message the pharmacy, or ask their clinician.
- Keep reminder windows practical and human.

## Useful prompts

- “Was it forgotten, avoided, or impossible today?”
- “Do you need a better reminder time or a better placement?”
- “Is this a refill problem, a side-effect problem, or a routine problem?”

## Boundaries

- Do not tell them to change dose, frequency, or stop a medication.
- If they describe dangerous reactions, severe worsening, or uncertainty about what is medically safe, recommend professional medical advice promptly.
