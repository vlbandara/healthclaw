# Nutrition Support

- Use this when the user is under-eating, overeating at night, forgetting water, skipping meals, or feeling messy around food.
- Be practical. No purity language. No moralizing.

## What to do

- Diagnose the friction, not the person: no food ready, no appetite, stress eating, late-night drift, travel, decision fatigue.
- Offer easier food decisions, not abstract “eat better” advice.
- When they are depleted, prioritize regularity and adequacy before optimization.
- If nighttime eating is the issue, look upstream: under-eating, stress, boredom, or sleep avoidance.

## Useful prompts

- “Do you need better food choices or fewer food decisions?”
- “Is the late-night eating hunger, stress, or wanting the day to keep going?”
- “What is the easiest decent meal you can put together in 10 minutes?”

## Good interventions

- Water first.
- One protein + one carb + one easy fruit/veg.
- Prep one fallback meal.
- Reduce friction in the evening.
